Details

Analyze tensile and compressive stress-strain test data by entering or uploading strain-stress values. The app automatically detects ultimate tensile strength (UTS) or ultimate compressive strength (UCS), estimates the elastic region, computes the elastic modulus from the best-fit linear segment, and generates a clean publication-style interactive graph.

Scientific Notes

Essential relationships

Stress: σ = F / A

Strain: ε = ΔL / L₀

Elastic modulus: E = Δσ / Δε in the initial linear region

Ultimate strength: maximum stress reached on the curve

Yield (approx.): the elastic → plastic transition; reported here as Yield Strain and Yield Stress at the end of the Elastic Region.

Energy absorbed: area under the stress–strain curve, ∫σ dε (shown as MPa·strain). This represents energy per unit volume absorbed up to a chosen strain.

Key graph features

Seating region: very low stress with slight fluctuations; machine/sample settling and surface contact (not true material behavior).

Elastic region: nearly linear increase; deformation is reversible and the elastic modulus is obtained from the best-fit line here.

Hardening region: stress continues to increase after yielding as the material strengthens/compacts or work-hardens; slope is typically lower than the elastic region but increases compared with the immediate post-yield segment.

Softening region: stress increases slowly or plateaus; internal structure collapses and energy absorption dominates (common in foams/polymers).

Densification region: sharp rise in stress; material becomes fully compacted and resistance increases rapidly.

Ultimate strength point: the highest stress on the curve.
